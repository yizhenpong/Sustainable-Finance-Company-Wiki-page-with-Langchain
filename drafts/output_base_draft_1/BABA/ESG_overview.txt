Header: ESG_overview /n  The 2022 Health for Humanity Report of the company highlights several Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) initiatives related to water efficiency, diversity, equity & inclusion, and living wages.

Regarding water efficiency, the company uses its Sustainability by Design Tool to ensure water-efficient processes in new plants, sets a target to reduce water use ratio by 20% by 2030, and focuses on improving water efficiency in Leadership Locations and Advanced Efficiency Locations. The report states that the company's highest achievement in water efficiency is a water use ratio of 1.79 liters of water used per liter of beverage in 2022, significantly better than the industry average for carbonated soft drinks (1.91 l/l of beverages).

In terms of diversity, equity & inclusion, the report provides data on gender representation and employment type across regions and job categories. The company is focused on achieving 100% regenerative water use across 175 facilities facing high levels of water stress by 2030, improving the health of 60 watersheds most critical for its operations and agricultural supply chain by 2030, and returning a cumulative total of 2 trillion liters of water to nature and communities globally over the life of its strategy.

The report also mentions the company's engagement on the topic of living wages, with plans to meet or exceed the BSR living wage benchmark in its own operations and develop a roadmap to progress toward a living wage where gaps exist. The company is collaborating with industry peers through AIM-Progress to advance responsible sourcing practices and promote living wages in global supply chains.

The report does not provide specific information on climate change resources, smarter chemistry engagement, or advocacy related to ESG initiatives.
